  1. Prep the Ginger: If your ginger is particularly "spicy" or old, soak the matchsticks in salted water for 5 minutes, then squeeze them dry. This mellows the bite so it doesn't overpower the meat.
  2. Sizzle the Aromatics: Heat oil in a wok or large pan over medium-high heat. Toss in the garlic and half of your ginger matchsticks. Fry until the garlic is golden and the ginger smells incredible.
  3. Sear the Chicken: Crank the heat up. Add the chicken and stir-fry until it’s no longer pink on the outside
  4. Sauce it Up: Pour in your sauce mixture (oyster sauce, fish sauce, sugar, soy). Stir constantly so the sugar caramelizes and coats every piece of chicken.
  5. The Final Toss: Add the rest of the ginger and your peppers/beans. Stir-fry for another 2–3 minutes. You want the vegetables to stay snappy, not mushy.
  6. Finish: Throw in the spring onions, give it one last quick toss, and kill the heat.
  • Chicken: 500g (Thighs are better for flavor, but breast works if you’re keeping it lean), cut into bite-sized pieces. Ginger: A massive chunk (about 100g). Peel it and julienne it into matchsticks. If you think it’s too much ginger, you’re doing it right. Garlic: 3–4 cloves, smashed and minced. Peppers: Long green beans or bell peppers (optional, for crunch). Spring Onions: 2 stalks, cut into 1-inch lengths.
